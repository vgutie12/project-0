My Life Purpose

 

I was only ten years old when the cardiologist diagnosed me with a severe heart condition called a “Coarctation in the aorta.” My Aorta vein was thinning out and if worst came to worst it could’ve cost me my life. The cardiologists let my parents know that I needed medical attention right away and the only solution to heal was through surgery. This year around this time marks fifteen years of having survived my heart surgery that my surgeon claimed I had only a 10% chance of surviving. I am a strong heart survivor who’s fought hard for her life, and will continue to do great things in order to repay for gods miracle.

My personal goal is to work for a non-profit organization that focuses on individuals who suffer from heart diseases such as Million Hearts, and Women-Heart. I feel this is my mission and it’s exactly what I was meant to do in my life. This is the reason to my survival. To inspire and motivate others who suffer from heart diseases like I once did. I will use social media, create websites, and blogs to raise awareness of my story. and others. The public can also save lives by volunteering and donating. My Passion

 

In the year 2015, I was lucky enough to find my passion for power and strength. I discovered weightlifting, and sure you can say I wanted to get stronger and build muscle. But as soon as my hands reached the weights, it made me want to push harder, and it challenged me to be a better version of myself mentally. I loved the fact that the gym was my escape from reality and how I was able to tune myself out from the world, and it was just me and my passion for lifting. That is where I preferred to be and stay in, my safe place.

For me, It was about becoming the woman I am meant to become. A strong woman, physically and mentally. In my lifting journey, everything began to fall into place in my life because I became more confident and sure of myself. The pride I felt in being able to challenge myself, learn, and train my body lifting regimes all by myself and becoming an inspiration to others. For me, lifting was also about healing. I used lifting as a way to heal any internal wounds I had. It helped me forget, forgive, and move on. I’m lucky to have found my passionate hobby; it changed my life for the better.
